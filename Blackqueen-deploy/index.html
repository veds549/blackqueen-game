Official game guide

Blackqueen Rulebook

1. Table setup

Blackqueen is played by 6 to 8 players using two standard 52-card decks with no jokers.

  • 6 players: 3 vs 3
  • 7 players: 3 vs 4
  • 8 players: 3 vs 5 or 4 vs 4

The host chooses the player count, team format, partner reveal rule, cards per player, and deck trimming before the hand begins.

2. Deck trimming

Every player must receive the same number of cards. The host may remove sets of 2s, 3s, 4s, 6s, and 7s. Fives cannot be removed because they carry points.

When an exact total needs part of a four-card set, the host may keep selected suits from the final removed set. Example: 7 players with 11 cards each requires 77 cards, so all 2s, 3s, and 4s are removed, one set of 6s is removed, and one selected 6 is kept.

3. Point cards

Every 5 5 points Every 10 10 points Every Ace 15 points Queen of Spades 30 points

Across both decks, the complete deck contains 300 points.

4. Dealing

The dealer starts with the player on the left and deals clockwise. The dealer may deal in any equal pattern, such as 1 card at a time, all cards together, or batches such as 7 and 7.

The deck is shuffled thoroughly before the first hand. Later hands receive only a light shuffle.

5. Bidding

Bidding begins with the player to the dealer's left. The minimum bid is 155 points and bids rise in steps of 5. Players may bid or pass. The highest remaining bidder wins the call.

6. Cut colour and partners

The bidder chooses the cut colour and two partner-card calls. Partners remain hidden until a called card is played.

Under First two shown, the first two matching called cards played reveal the partners. Under One of each, one player is claimed by each distinct called card.

A player who reveals multiple valid partner cards becomes a double or triple partner. The bidder may also gain additional partner multipliers by holding and playing called cards.

7. Trick play

The bidder leads the first trick. Players must follow the led suit when possible. A player without that suit may play any card, including the cut colour.

The highest cut-colour card wins the trick. If no cut card is played, the highest card of the led suit wins. Rank order is Ace, King, Queen, Jack, 10 down to 2. With duplicate equal cards, the first one played remains higher.

The trick winner collects its point cards and leads the next trick.

8. Scoring

If the bidding team captures at least its bid, the bidder and partners each receive the bid value. If the call is broken, bidding-team members lose the bid value and every non-bidder receives half the bid, rounded to a whole number.

A double or triple partner's award or loss is multiplied by that partner count. Example: on a successful 180 bid, a double partner receives 360. On a failed bid, that player receives -360.
